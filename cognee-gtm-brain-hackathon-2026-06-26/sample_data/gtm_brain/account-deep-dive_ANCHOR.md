# Clay — the anchor account

> **Why this is the anchor:** Clay is the single highest-conviction sponsor in the dataset, and it is
> *real* — so when you connect Clay / Exa / web search, the firmographics below light up live. The
> account also closes the loop on the brand thesis: Clay literally created and funds the **"GTM
> engineer"** category that GTM Tech Week is named after. Everything here is web-verified
> (2026-06-22); unverifiable items are marked `[unconfirmed]`.

**Bottom line.** Clay is a **$3.1B GTM platform** that **opened its first-ever international office in
London in March 2026** because Europe is already ~20% of revenue and they had "almost no one on the
ground." They are hiring a *founding* EMEA GTM team right now, run their own conference (Sculpt) and
60+ community clubs, and built their brand on GTM engineering. A GTM event in London is on their
critical path. The right move is **co-create a GTM-engineering track, not sell a booth.**

## 1. What they do
GTM data-orchestration + automation: find, enrich and act on customer data by "waterfalling" 130+
providers, run AI research agents (**Claygent**), score accounts, track signals, and auto-write
outreach — all no-code. Positioning: *"Clay is for go-to-market."*
Source: <https://www.clay.com/blog/clay-raises-62m-to-turn-any-growth-idea-into-reality>

## 6. The sponsor pitch (grounded in the above)
1. **They just planted a flag in London and need to fill it** — first office, 20% of revenue, founding
   team hiring now. A London GTM event is the most efficient awareness+pipeline play they have.
2. **They own the category the event is named after** — sponsoring "GTM Tech Week" is on-message and
   brand-defensive.
3. **Events are a funded, proven motion for them** — Sculpt + 60 clubs + a fund that reimburses event
   spend. Pitch a co-hosted track, not a banner.
4. **Capital + mandate are aligned right now** — fresh $100M, explicit "go all-in on Europe." The
   timing *is* the pitch.
