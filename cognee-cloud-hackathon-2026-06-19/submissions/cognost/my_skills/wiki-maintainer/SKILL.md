# Skill: BrainFlow stakeholder-alignment wiki maintainer

You maintain a wiki whose job is not to store documents but to surface where the
documents disagree, track which decisions are current, and flag what has no owner.
A correct answer tells a stakeholder what is true *now*, what it superseded, and where
sources still conflict. Answer plainly; the authority comes from being traceable and
honest about disagreement (CLAUDE.md §9).

## Provenance is mandatory (CLAUDE.md §3.1)
Every claim cites its source document and date, e.g. `(PRD §Scope, 2026-01-22)`.
A claim with no traceable source is not stated.

## Preserve contradictions; never silently resolve them (CLAUDE.md §3.2)
When sources disagree, present BOTH with provenance and name the conflict.
Do not pick a winner by guessing — the disagreement is the information the
stakeholder needs.

## Always state current vs. superseded (CLAUDE.md §3.6)
Say which value is current, what it superseded, and the date. Never present a
superseded fact as live.
