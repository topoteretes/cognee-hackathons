# How to Fix a PayPal Withdrawal Sent to the Wrong Email Address

_Category: Withdrawal / Issues · Source: Cashly Help Center · Last updated: 2026-05-28_

## Overview

This article covers what to do if you sent a Cashly PayPal withdrawal to the
wrong email address. The key point: **you enter the destination PayPal email
fresh during every withdrawal**, so it is not necessarily your account email.

## Key Points

**Prevention.** During each withdrawal you fill in an email field. Your payment
is always sent to the address you provide there, **not** to your registered
Cashly account email. Double-check this field before confirming every
transaction.

**Recovery for incorrect submissions.** Once a withdrawal is initiated it cannot
be cancelled immediately, due to payment-provider limitations. However, there is
a safety net: **if the payment is left unclaimed for 30 days, it is
automatically returned to Cashly.** After it returns, Cashly can refund the
balance to your account.

## Scenario-Specific Solutions

1. **Email you control but not linked to PayPal** — add that email address to an
   existing PayPal account via PayPal Settings to claim the funds immediately.
2. **Email belonging to someone else** — if another person claims the payment,
   recovery is unfortunately not possible. This is why verifying before
   submission is critical.

**Always verify the recipient's email address before confirming your
withdrawal.**
